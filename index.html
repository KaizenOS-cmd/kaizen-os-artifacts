First Principles Thinking
Boil it down. Build back up. Own the insight.
Most people think by analogy — copying what already exists. First principles thinking means stripping a problem down to its fundamental truths, then rebuilding from scratch. It's how Musk concluded that rockets didn't have to cost $65M. Raw materials cost $2M. The rest was assumption.
🔩 The Analogy Think of your beliefs as a house built on rented land. Analogy thinking = renovating the existing rooms. First principles = asking why the house is even there, tearing it down, and building on land you actually own. The structure becomes unshakeable because it's yours.
The three-step drill:
1. Identify the assumptions — what does everyone "just know" about this?
2. Break them apart — which are actually true? Which are inherited?
3. Reconstruct — what's the simplest, most powerful version of the solution?
🧠 Daily Reprogramming Question Pick one belief you hold about your career, your industry, or your potential. Ask: "Is this actually true, or did I just inherit it?" Write the answer. That gap is your leverage.
